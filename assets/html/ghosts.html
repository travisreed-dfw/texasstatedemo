Ghosts aren't real.

Ghosts, often depicted as spectral figures or disembodied spirits in folklore and popular media, are widely regarded by the scientific community as unreal. There is no empirical evidence to support the existence of ghosts, and many reported sightings can typically be attributed to psychological phenomena, such as the mind's tendency to perceive patterns or human-like figures in random stimuli. Environmental factors, like unusual lighting, drafts, and the settling of buildings, can create sensory effects that might be misinterpreted as supernatural occurrences. Furthermore, the belief in ghosts can be influenced by cultural narratives, personal expectations, and emotional states. Scientifically controlled experiments attempting to demonstrate the existence of ghosts have consistently failed to produce verifiable results, reinforcing the prevailing scientific consensus that ghosts, as they are commonly understood, do not exist.
